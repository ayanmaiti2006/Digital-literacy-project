# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist

As a Student Digital Ambassador, I have developed this guide to help my peers recognize and avoid common digital threats in India.

### 🛡️ General Cyber Hygiene
1. [cite_start]**Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Always use 2FA on your college email, social media, and GitHub accounts to add an extra layer of security. [cite: 82]
2. **Use Strong, Unique Passwords:** Avoid using easily guessable information like your name or birthdate. [cite_start]Use a mix of symbols, numbers, and cases. [cite: 82]
3. [cite_start]**Think Before You Click:** Be wary of shortened URLs or emails claiming you have won a prize or scholarship that requires immediate action. [cite: 82]
4. [cite_start]**Keep Software Updated:** Regularly update your mobile OS and apps to ensure you have the latest security patches against malware. [cite: 82]
5. [cite_start]**Secure Public Wi-Fi Use:** Avoid logging into bank accounts or sensitive portals when using free campus or public Wi-Fi without a VPN. [cite: 82]
6. [cite_start]**Social Media Privacy:** Regularly audit your "tagged" photos and friend requests to ensure your personal life isn't visible to strangers or potential scammers. [cite: 82]

### 💰 Financial & UPI Safety
7. **PIN is for Sending, Not Receiving:** Remember that entering your UPI PIN is only required to *deduct* money from your account. [cite_start]Never enter your PIN to "receive" a payment or a refund. [cite: 83]
8. [cite_start]**Verify the Receiver:** Before completing any UPI transaction, double-check the verified name displayed on the app to ensure it matches the person or merchant you intend to pay. [cite: 83]
