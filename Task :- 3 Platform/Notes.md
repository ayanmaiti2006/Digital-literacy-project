### Task 3: Explore Coding & Collaboration Platforms

[cite_start]As a **Student Digital Ambassador**, this task focuses on your ability to guide peers toward the right online tools for studying, practicing, and collaborating[cite: 4, 44]. [cite_start]It is divided into two parts: coding practice and cloud-based collaboration[cite: 45].

#### 💻 Part A: Coding Practice
* [cite_start]**Goal**: Build familiarity with professional coding practice platforms[cite: 45, 46].
* **Requirements**:
    * [cite_start]Create an account on **HackerRank**, **CodeChef**, or **HackerEarth**[cite: 47].
    * [cite_start]Complete at least one beginner-level challenge (e.g., HackerRank's 'Solve Me First' or Python Introduction)[cite: 48, 49].
    * [cite_start]**Submission**: Take a screenshot of the completed challenge and your badge/score to save in the `task-3-platforms/` folder[cite: 50].

#### 🤝 Part B: Google Workspace Collaboration
* [cite_start]**Goal**: Master cloud collaboration tools for academic and peer support[cite: 44, 51].
* **Requirements**:
    * [cite_start]Create a **Google Form** titled "Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz"[cite: 52].
    * [cite_start]The form must contain exactly **5 questions**[cite: 52].
    * [cite_start]Include at least one **multiple-choice** question and one **short-answer** question[cite: 53].
    * [cite_start]**Submission**: Share the form link in your `README.md` and add screenshots of both the form and the linked Google Sheet (response view) to your repository[cite: 54].

#### 📝 Project Report Requirements
In your written report (150-200 words), you must describe:
* [cite_start]Which platforms you chose to use[cite: 55].
* [cite_start]What specific tasks you built or practiced[cite: 55].
* [cite_start]How these tools will assist you in your academic career[cite: 55].

> [cite_start]**Tip**: This task is worth **20 marks** toward your internal assessment[cite: 43]. [cite_start]The Google Form you create is a real deliverable meant to help your batchmates in your role as an ambassador[cite: 56, 57].
